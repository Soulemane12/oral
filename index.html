Learn About Oral Hygiene

Proper Brushing Technique

1

Apply a pea-sized amount of toothpaste to your toothbrush.

2

Hold the toothbrush at a 45-degree angle to your gums.

3

Use gentle, circular motions to brush the outer, inner, and chewing surfaces of each tooth.

4

Brush for at least two minutes, ensuring all areas are covered.

5

Don't forget to brush your tongue to remove bacteria and freshen breath.

Effective Flossing Techniques

1

Take about 18 inches of dental floss and wind most of it around each middle finger.

2

Hold the floss tightly between your thumbs and forefingers.

3

Guide the floss between your teeth using a gentle rubbing motion.

4

Curve the floss around each tooth in a C-shape and slide it beneath the gumline.

5

Use a clean section of floss for each tooth to avoid spreading bacteria.

Nutrition for Healthy Teeth and Gums

1

Consume a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains.

2

Limit sugary snacks and beverages that can contribute to tooth decay.

3

Include calcium-rich foods like dairy products, almonds, and leafy greens to strengthen enamel.

4

Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water, which helps wash away food particles and bacteria.

5

Incorporate vitamin C sources such as oranges, strawberries, and bell peppers to maintain healthy gums.
